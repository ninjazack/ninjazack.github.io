Master's Thesis Proposal

High-Speed Rail Expansion and Electric Vehicle Adoption in Spain

Supervised by Prof. Javier Gardeazabal.

This thesis asks whether access to high-speed rail affects local electric vehicle adoption in Spain. It adapts a recent China-focused research question to the Spanish setting, where high-speed rail geography, rollout timing, and institutions differ.

The project constructs a municipality-year panel for 2015-2025, combining DGT vehicle registrations with high-speed rail station and line data, municipal boundaries, socioeconomic controls, and policy and charging-infrastructure data.

Methodologically, the thesis is centered on causal inference and policy evaluation. The empirical strategy uses staggered Difference-in-Differences and Callaway-Sant'Anna DID estimators, supported by balance checks, pre-trend/event-study diagnostics, a least-cost instrumental-variable robustness exercise, and mechanism checks related to policy exposure and charging infrastructure.

More broadly, the project reflects my interest in credible empirical designs for policy-relevant economic questions.
